Understanding Your Cognitive Assessment

CDA Metrics (0-100% scale):
  • Adaptability: Measures how well you adjust when difficulty changes. Higher scores indicate better adaptation to new challenge levels.
  • Consistency: Reflects stability in your performance. Higher scores mean more predictable, steady results across questions.
  • Recovery Rate: Shows your ability to bounce back after level drops. Higher scores indicate strong recovery from setbacks.
  • Error Persistence: Indicates tendency for consecutive errors. Lower scores are better (showing you learn from mistakes quickly).
  • Knowledge Mastery: Your mastery level by category, calculated from accuracy and recent performance trends.
Executive Function Indicators (0-100% scale):
  • Processing Speed: How quickly you respond relative to your average. Higher scores indicate faster, efficient processing.
  • Impulsivity Control: Ability to avoid hasty, incorrect answers. Higher scores show better self-control and careful thinking.
  • Analytical Thinking: Tendency to take time for thoughtful, correct answers. Higher scores indicate deeper analytical processing.
  • Cognitive Endurance: Ability to maintain performance throughout the session. Higher scores show sustained focus and energy.
  • Self Regulation: Capacity to recover and adapt after difficulties. Higher scores indicate strong self-management skills.
Score Interpretation:
  • 70-100%: Strong performance (Green indicator)
  • 40-69%: Moderate performance (Amber indicator)
  • 0-39%: Developing area (Red indicator)

Reference: Based on Cognitive Diagnostic Assessment (Rule Space Method) by Tatsuoka, K. K. (2009). These metrics provide insights beyond simple accuracy scores, identifying specific cognitive strengths and learning patterns.
